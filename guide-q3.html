Question 3: What Have You Done Outside School to Prepare?

This section helps show your initiative and real-world engagement with medicine. It’s a great place to show maturity, independence, and reflective thinking.

1. Structure Your Response

  • Overview: Briefly outline what you’ve done (e.g. shadowing, volunteering, online courses)
  • Depth: Focus on a few meaningful activities, not a list
  • Reflection: Describe what each taught you about medicine or yourself
  • Application: Link what you learned to the skills needed in medical school or practice

2. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Listing all your activities with no reflection
  • Talking about generic soft skills like "communication" without evidence
  • Using cliché phrases like "it confirmed my desire to be a doctor" without explaining how

3. Tips for High Impact

  • Be specific: describe what you saw, did, or felt
  • Talk about challenges or ethical situations you encountered
  • Include informal learning too — books, podcasts, virtual experiences

4. Example Segment

“During a GP placement, I was struck by how much time was spent explaining and reassuring rather than diagnosing. I learned that medicine is as much about managing uncertainty and emotions as it is about science — and that this requires empathy and patience.”

Use this question to prove you're not just curious — you're proactive, thoughtful, and already developing the mindset of a future doctor.
