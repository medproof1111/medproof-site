Question 1: Why Do You Want to Study Medicine?

This is your opportunity to show admissions tutors your motivation for becoming a doctor. Go beyond clichés like "I want to help people" and provide insight into your journey, your values, and what draws you to medicine.

1. Structure Your Response

  • Opening hook: A compelling story or moment that sparked your interest
  • Exploration: Reflect on experiences (e.g. shadowing, volunteering)
  • Commitment: Show that this is a well-informed, long-term decision
  • Conclusion: Link your passion to your future as a student and doctor

2. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Vague statements like "I've always wanted to be a doctor" with no evidence
  • Overused buzzwords without reflection (e.g. “passion” or “empathy”)
  • Overly dramatic stories that don’t clearly relate to your motivation
  • Listing experiences without showing what you learned from them

3. Tips for High Impact

  • Show, don’t just tell – use vivid moments to demonstrate insight
  • Include a variety of influences (academic, personal, extracurricular)
  • Keep your voice honest, reflective, and specific

4. Example Opening

“Watching a junior doctor calmly lead a resuscitation in A&E, I felt a mix of admiration and curiosity. It wasn’t the glamour that drew me, but the combination of knowledge, teamwork and steady compassion under pressure. That moment made me realise I wanted to be the one making those calls – and saving lives.”

Use this question to show your thinking, not just your experiences. Medicine is a tough and rewarding path – this is where you show you understand both sides.
