Question 2: How Have Your Studies and Qualifications Prepared You?

This question is about more than just listing your subjects. It's about showing how your education has given you the tools, mindset, and motivation to pursue medicine.

1. Structure Your Response

  • Academic foundation: Talk about relevant subjects (e.g. Biology, Chemistry) and what skills they gave you
  • Independent learning: Reflect on how you approach problem-solving and self-directed study
  • Critical thinking: Give examples of projects, coursework or topics that developed your analytical skills
  • Motivation link: Explain how academic experiences confirmed your desire to study medicine

2. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Just listing subjects and grades
  • Focusing too much on one subject (e.g. Biology only)
  • Forgetting to explain how your studies relate to the demands of medicine

3. Tips for High Impact

  • Include a variety of examples: science subjects, coursework, EPQ, academic challenges
  • Reflect on feedback you’ve received and how you improved
  • Show how your mindset matches that of a medical student: curiosity, resilience, growth

4. Example Segment

“Studying chemistry taught me how to think logically through complex problems, while biology developed my ability to understand systems. But it was my EPQ on gene editing ethics that made me realise how much I enjoy grappling with moral dilemmas — a crucial skill for a future doctor.”

This is your chance to show how you think and how you learn — two things every successful medical student needs to thrive.
